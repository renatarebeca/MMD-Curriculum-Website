Internship


Internship

Internships take place in the fourth semester and must correspond to 15 ECTS. An internship period (of a minimum duration of 10 weeks) is a mandatory part of all the programmes. It provides to the students the chance to test their knowledge and skills in an authentic work environment during their study. Enterprises have a chance of directly influencing future employees in the business and trying out potential employees, and students often provide a fresh input to an enterprise.

Doing internship abroad is a unique opportunity to develop professionally by engaging in a different work culture. At a personal level an internship abroad offers the students a rare chance of experiencing another country in a different way than they would as a regular tourist and it will help them create an international network.

Rules on completion

The internship can form the basis of the student’s final project and can be organised in a flexible and differentiated manner. Students can choose to combine internships in two or more organisations or different types of internships. The conditions governing internships are described in more detail on the learning platform for the Multimedia Design and Communication programme before the fourth semester begins.

The internship is unpaid.

Based on the general learning objectives for the internship period, students develop their own individual learning objectives for their internship in cooperation with the host company and the institution.

During the internship, students are assigned a supervisor from the programme and a contact/supervisor from the host organisation.

The internship is concluded with an exam (internship exam).



How to get started

To apply for an internship position is very like a job application process. You need to actively research your options based on your wishes and competences and apply accordingly. We advise you to start this process early in your studies to ensure your success.

KEA can help you with advice about CV, motivation letter and finding the right internship for you.

Students are required to complete a Training Agreement before departure reflecting relevant tasks and learning goals. Internships are an integrated part of all full-time courses and count for 15 or 30 ECTS, depending on the course. The internship includes a mid-way and final evaluation and assessment.

You can apply for an ERASMUS grant if you spend your internship at an enterprise or organisation within the European Union, EU candidate countries or EFTA/EEA countries.
