Introduction


Main


A multimedia designer is an artist of integrating multiple forms of media. Therefore he has an important role in creating websites, video games, information kiosks and many other interactive applications. Careers in multimedia design can be found in a growing number of industries, from small agencies to large companies all over the world.
KEAâ€™s multimedia design programme is based on 4 core areas: Design and visualization, Interaction development, Communication and presentation and Business.
You are provided with theory and assignments on designing web portals, mobile applications, digital communication and games in addition to digital video, animation and frontend programming.
During your studies you get familiar with theories and methods on the field of multimedia design, interaction and communication. You participate in teams to fulfill multimedia tasks from a concept, through visualization and coding, until the end product, where you present your production to stakeholders.
With the elective subject, you get the possibility to specialize on one or two areas, while on the internship you get to practice all your acquired competencies in cooperation with real companies or organizations.


Core areas

Design and visualization

This area teaches the student how to apply multimedia design and concept development principles. It also includes graphic design, interactive user interface design, aesthetics, video/audio and animation. In addition, the student learns to integrate different types of digital media on the digital platforms.

Interaction development

This area teaches the student to program dynamic multimedia applications. The student is also to obtain a basic understanding of system development. Furthermore, the student is to acquire the skills needed to structure and implement complex dynamic multimedia applications with the integration of databases.

Communication and presentation

This area teaches the student the skills how to handle communication in multimedia tasks. It also includes handling communication channels and opportunities in complex organisations and networks, both locally and globally.

The Business

This area gives the students the tools to use organisational aspects in multimedia development, and the means to plan, manage and carry out development projects.

  • animation techniques
  • narrative and production techniques
Skills
  • ideation and concept development
  • design user interfaces for digital platforms
  • key user-oriented design methods
  • prepare graphic material for further production
  • present design-related problems and possible solutions

Competencies
